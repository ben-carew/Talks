The Dark Matter Coincidence

Ben Carew - Neutrinos and Cosmology Seminar

What’s Dark Matter?

  • Interacts gravitationally - 80% of total mass in the universe
  • No (or very weak) electromagnetic interactions
  • Forms halo structures around galaxies, including the Milky Way
  • Mostly non-baryonic → beyond the Standard Model
  • Moves slowly, i.e. “cold”
  • No confirmed detection via non-gravitational effects

The Dark Matter Coincidence

\[\Omega_{DM} \sim \Omega_{VM}\]

  • Observed dark matter mass density is roughly 5 times the visible matter mass density
  • Why is the ratio of DM to VM density in the universe on the order of unity?
  • Implies a deep connection between dark and visible sectors
  • Answer contains clues for the nature of dark matter, and matter-antimatter asymmetry in the universe, and DM-VM coupling
  • Most standard DM candidate theories (WIMPs, axions, etc.) predict \[\Omega_{DM}/\Omega_{VM} \sim O(1)\]

Model Building Approach

  • Two sub-problems: \[n_{DM} \sim n_{VM}\] and \[m_{DM} \sim m_{VM}\]
  • Number density: Asymmetric Dark Matter (ADM)
  • Mass: dark QCD (dQCD)
  • Aim: Find dQCD models that satisfy these conditions:
    • Produce similar DM and VM baryon masses
    • Can be easily embedded into a ADM model
    • Does not breach current collider constraints
    • Does not cause naturalness issues
  • Look for models with infra-red fixed points (IRFPs) to relate \(\Lambda_{QCD}\) to \(\Lambda_{dQCD}\)

The Model Framework

  • Ingredients: dark QCD (light dark quarks) + new fields charged under QCD and dQCD
  • Dirac fermion fields
  • Complex scalar fields
  • All new fields have heavy mass M
  • \[m_{DM}\sim m_{VM} \rightarrow \Lambda_{dQCD} \sim \Lambda_{QCD} \rightarrow \alpha_s \sim \alpha_d\] below confinement scale
  • Running of coupling constants → Renormalisation group \(\beta\) functions
  • Need to find \(\beta\) coefficients that lead to fixed point in IR and divergence in UV
  • Light dark quarks are dirac fermions with mass below the confinement scale
  • Dark quarks combine to form a dark baryon -> DM candidate. Needs to be ~1GeV

Infrared Fixed Points

  • Fixed point: \(\beta_v = \beta_d = 0\)
  • 4 possible solutions:
    • \(\alpha^*_s = 0, \alpha^*_d = 0\)
    • \(\alpha^*_s =-4\pi\frac{b_v^{(0)}}{b_v^{(1)}}, \alpha_d = 0\)
    • \(\alpha^*_s = 0, \alpha^*_d = -4\pi\frac{b_v^{(0)}}{b_v^{(1)}}\)
    • \(\alpha_s = 4\pi\frac{b_v^{(0)}b_d^{(1)}-b_v^{(1’)}b_d^{(0)}}{b_v^{(1’)}b_d^{(1’)}-b_v^{(1)}b_d^{(1)}}, \alpha_d = 4\pi\frac{b_d^{(0)}b_v^{(1)}-b_d^{(1’)}b_v^{(0)}}{b_v^{(1’)}b_d^{(1’)}-b_v^{(1)}b_d^{(1)}}\)
  • Consider two possibilities:
    • finite coupling fixed point (FCFP)
    • zero coupling fixed point (ZCFP)
  • Judge models with \(\epsilon_v\), the proportion of UV parameter space with \(0.2 \text{ GeV} < \Lambda_{dQCD} < 5 \text{ GeV}\)
  • \(\epsilon_v > 0.3\) = valid model

Attempt #1: Finite Coupling Fixed Point

  • \(\alpha\)s depend non-trivially on \(\beta\)-function coefficients
  • Perturbativity → specific coefficients →most models ruled out
  • Valid fields have very small coefficients
  • Predicts many new O(100GeV) mass coloured particles, would be produced at the LHC
  • For M > 1 TeV, require strong running of \(\alpha\)s →high field multiplicities
  • Total Models
    • Has Fixed Point: 12288

    • And viable: 155

Attempt #2: Zero Coupling Fixed Point

  • Requirement: 1-loop \(\beta\) coefficients are positive
  • No precise cancellations
  • Benefits:
    • Many models have ZCFP → more general viable models
    • Even in most basic model: 6 quarks + 1 dark quark + 1 double-charged fermion
    • Achieve ZCFPs with small field multiplicities (unlike FCFPs)
    • Gauge coupling runnings are generically strong, so M > ~TeV →avoid collider constraints
  • Problem: new heavy particles in 10s-100s of TeV range contribute to Higgs mass running → naturalness problem

Introducing Supersymmetry

  • Heavy new particles contribute to Hiss mass parameter running -> naturalness problem
  • Solution: Supersymmetry (SUSY)
  • Prevents the running of Higgs mass
  • SUSY threshold = 5 TeV. Below running is unchanged, above running of \(\alpha_s\) and \(\alpha_d\) separate
  • SUSY fields are chiral supermultiplets, equivalent to one dirac fermion and two complex scalars
  • SUSY reduces multiplicity of new fields to produce ZCFP
  • SUSY running is weaker than SM running, so detachment happens later

Embedding in Asymmetric Dark Matter

  • dQCD models solve \(m_{DM}\sim m_{VM}\), but still need \(n_{DM}\sim n_{VM}\)
  • Bais & Schwaller mechanism for asymmetry
  • Heavy right-handed neutrinos decay asymmetrically into jointly-charged field
  • Jointly charged field decays into DM and VM, distributes equal asymmetry to each
  • Naturally obtain \(n_{DM}\sim n_{VM}\)
  • However, heavy neutrinos asymmetrically couple to leptons
  • Leptogenesis generates baryons which carry lepton asymmetry
  • Weakens DM and VM asymmetry relationship
  • Need the symmetric components of DM to efficiently annihilate

Embedding in Asymmetric Dark Matter

  • Non-SUSY
  • Number density ratio depends on the asymmetry in jointly-charged field and leptons
  • \(\frac{n_B}{n_D} = \frac{8}{23}(1-\frac{\Delta n_L}{\Delta n_{\Phi}})\)
  • If asymmetries \(\Delta n_L\) and \(\Delta n_{\Phi}\) are similar, cancellation
  • \(n_{B}/n_{D} << O(1)\)
  • Only if yukawa couplings aren’t same size
  • For low-mass viable models, we get \(n_{B}/n_{D} \sim O(1)\)
  • Lepton coupling with heavy neutrinos is not a problem
  • If:
    • Heavy neutrinos explain SM neutrino masses with seesaw mechanism
    • New fields couple with strength \(O(1)\)
  • then \(n_{B}/n_{D} << O(1)\)

  • Direct Detection
    • Modified DM-nucleon interactions

Stochastic Gravitational Waves

  • dQCD with sufficient light dark quarks →phase transition in primordial dark quark plasma
  • Generates stochastic micro-Hertz GW production
  • Possibly detectable at proposed future stochastic GW detectors
  • Viability calculations made with EFT in highly non-perturbative scenarios, not high accuracy

Conclusion

  • Dynamical mechanism -> relates confinement scales of dQCD and QCD
  • Embed in ADM theory -> relates masses and number density -> relates mass density
  • Running couplings: fixed point in IR, run to non-perturbative region -> confinement
  • ZCFP improves on FCFP, as high field multiplicities are not required
  • SUSY models gave promising fractions of valid models
  • Potential signals with direct detection, collider searches or GW detection
