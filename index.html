Double Higgs Production

Ben Carew

BSM Seminar

16/05/24

- pp -> hh not yet observed - Double higgs production is a very rare process in the SM, a facor 1000 less likely than single higg production - Measurement of the double higgs production rate could be an effective probe for new physics, since the SM rate is so small - In this talk I will motivate why double higgs decay is interesting, why it is a good probe for new physics, and discuss the theoretical and experimental challenges

Motivation

\[V = \frac{m^2_h}{2}h^2 + \textcolor{orange}{\lambda_3}h^3 + \textcolor{cyan}{\lambda_4}h^4\]

with \(\textcolor{orange}{\lambda_3^{SM}}=\frac{m^2_h}{2v}=0.13v\), and \(\textcolor{cyan}{\lambda_4^{SM}}=\frac{h^2}{8v^2}=0.03\)

  • SM predicts weak Higgs self-couplings
  • \(\textcolor{orange}{\lambda_3}\) can only be probed by measuring double Higgs production rates
- We want to measure the parameters of the Higgs potential to verify that electroweak symmetry breaking occurs in nature in the way that the SM predicts - The self-couplings in the SM are very weak - $v$ is the higgs energy vacuum expectation value - the only way to directly measure \lambda_3 is by measuring double higgs production

Gluon Fusion

\[A^{\mu\nu}_{AB}=\frac{\alpha_s}{8\pi v^2}\delta_{AB}\left[P^{\mu\nu}_0(p_1,p_2)\textcolor{orange}{\hat{F}_1}(s,t,u,m_t^2)+P^{\mu\nu}_2(p_1,p_2,p_3)\textcolor{cyan}{\hat{F}_2}(s,t,u,m_t^2)\right]\]

- Double higgs production rate is dependent on: - BSM coloured particles at loop-order - cubic higgs coupling constant - Top quark Yukawa couplings (coupling to higgs) - gluon fusion is the dominant process in pp->hh - Leading contribution of gg->hh is top quark loop.

\[A^{\mu\nu}_{AB}=\frac{\alpha_s}{8\pi v^2}\delta_{AB}\left[P^{\mu\nu}_0(p_1,p_2)\textcolor{orange}{\hat{F}_1}(s,t,u,m_t^2)+P^{\mu\nu}_2(p_1,p_2,p_3)\textcolor{cyan}{\hat{F}_2}(s,t,u,m_t^2)\right]\]

Assuming for now $\textcolor{red}{m_t}>>\textcolor{lightgreen}{s}$:

\[\textcolor{orange}{\hat{F}_1}(s,t,u,m_t^2) = \textcolor{yellow}{\hat{F}_1^{tri}}(s,t,u,m_t^2)+\textcolor{fuchsia}{\hat{F}_1^{box}}(s,t,u,m_t^2)\]

\(\textcolor{yellow}{\hat{F}^{tri}_1}(s,t,u,m_t^2) = \frac{4m_h^2}{\textcolor{lightgreen}{s}-m_h^2}\textcolor{lightgreen}{s}\left(\frac{\lambda_3}{\lambda_3^{SM}}\right)\), and \(\textcolor{fuchsia}{\hat{F}^{box}_1}(s,t,u,m_t^2) = -\frac{4}{3}\textcolor{lightgreen}{s}\)

\[\textcolor{cyan}{\hat{F}_2}(s,t,u,m_t^2) = -\frac{11}{45}\textcolor{lightgreen}{s}\frac{p^2_T}{\textcolor{red}{m^2_t}}\]

Everything vanishes at the energy threshold, $\sqrt{\textcolor{lightgreen}{s}}=2m_h$ and $\textcolor{red}{m_t}\rightarrow \infty$.

- P_0 and P_2 are projectors of the spin 0 and spin 2 states respectively - spin can be 0 or 2 from $h\rightarrow\gamma\gamma$, photon spins add/cancel - s, t, u are mandelstam variables, p_T is transverse higgs momentum - F functions have known analytical solutions. The largest contributions are from top quark loops - the F functions vanish at the limit s=, which means we cannot make this assumption and get useful results

Kinematic Distribution

- Perform calculations by expanding in powers of the top mass does not work, due to large contributions from terms containing s/m_t^2.

A Better Approach

  1. Compute \(K = \frac{d\sigma_{NNLO}}{d\sigma_{LO}}\) at the limit of \(\textcolor{orange}{m_t \rightarrow \infty}\)
  2. Calculate the leading order term with finite \(\textcolor{cyan}{m_t}\)
  3. Use $K$ to rescale the leading order term and calculate NLO with $m_t$ effects

Using this method, the exact NLO term for double Higgs production has been computed with all finite $m_t$ effects!

Experimental Progress

Prediction for HL-LHC with $L=\text{3 ab}^{-1}$ will be sensitive to $\frac{\lambda_3}{\lambda_3^{SM}} > 8.7$, and $\frac{\lambda_3}{\lambda_3^{SM}} < -1.3$

There's a long way to go!

- These limits are from LHC 8 TeV data - ATLAS has predicted that with the LHC luminosity upgrade will improve limits, but not enough - This is one of the main motivators for building a 100TeV collider, to improve these limits

BSM Models

\[gg\rightarrow \textcolor{orange}{X} \rightarrow \textcolor{cyan}{hh}\]

  • Proposed new particle(s): Heavy "new Higgs", neutral scalar
  • Decays to 2 SM Higgs particles with $\sim 30\%$ BR
  • Models predict $s$-channel resonance
  • Interference from SM Higgs production
- Plot shows a Z_2 singlet model, adds a heavy neutral scalar particle with mass ~300GeV - The plot structure is due to s-channel resonance (peak), and from SM higgs production interference (trough) - These BSM models can have branching ratios up to 30%, which is significant and measurable

- Plot shows LHC limits on double higgs production through new physics mechanisms with resonant decays - Plotted as a function of the new physics mass scale

Another Solution?

  • It may be possible to measure $\lambda_3$ from electroweak radiative corrections on single Higgs production rates
  • $\lambda_3$ appears in 2-loop order of $gg\rightarrow h$ and 1-loop in other rarer production mechanisms
  • The high-luminosity LHC upgrade will not significantly improve the BSM constraints from this method

\[2\sigma: -9.4<\frac{\lambda_3}{\lambda_3^{SM}}<16\]

Summary

  • Higgs physics has 2 goals:
    • to test the SM electroweak symmetry breaking for SM deviations through the Higgs potential parameters
    • To search for hints of new particles due to non-SM decays or cross sections
  • Theory challenges: QCD renders perturbation theory ineffective, need new theory methods for calculating $>$NLO terms
  • Experimental challenges: Many BSM models are allowed, need better limits - and therefore a bigger collider!
